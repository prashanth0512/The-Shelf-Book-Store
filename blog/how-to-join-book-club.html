How to Join Our Book Club — Everything You Need to Know

Sneha Kulkarni • Nov 05, 2026 • 5 min read
Back to The Bookmark

If you've never been to a book club before, it can feel intimidating. What if you don't have smart things to say? What if you didn't love the book everyone else adored? What if you only got a third of the way through and had to fake it?

We are here to tell you: all of that is completely fine. In fact, the best book club conversations we've ever hosted have begun with someone saying, "I didn't finish it, but here's what bothered me even in the first fifty pages..."

"A book club is not a test. It's a conversation. And the most memorable conversations start with honest, unpolished reactions."

Which Club Is Right for You?

We run six genre-specific clubs at The Shelf, each meeting once a month. Here's a quick guide:

Mystery & Thriller Circle: For fans of Tana French, Richard Osman, and anything with a twist that makes you gasp. Meets on the second Friday of every month.

Literary Fiction Forum: Booker shortlists, debut novels, and books that defy easy categorisation. Meets on the first Wednesday.

Fantasy & Sci-Fi Guild: Epic series, world-building obsessives, and people who own more than three maps of fictional continents. Meets on the third Saturday.

Non-Fiction Salon: Narrative non-fiction, biography, history, and science writing. Meets on the fourth Thursday.

How to Join

Simply register via our website or visit us in-store. Literary Members and Bibliophile Club members attend all meetings free of charge. Non-members can attend a first session free as a trial. The only thing you need to bring is yourself — and, ideally, a copy of the book.

Your first meeting will feel like joining a group of friends who've known each other for years — because, in a sense, you already have something in common with everyone in the room: you all read the same book. That's more than enough to start.

Sneha Kulkarni

Sneha is our Events Coordinator at The Shelf, and has facilitated over 150 literary events since joining us in 2023. She believes that the best book club discussions always start with wild disagreement.
