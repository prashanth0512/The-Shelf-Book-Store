The Top Reads of 2026 — Our Booksellers' Definitive List

Priya Nair • Nov 20, 2026 • 8 min read
Back to The Bookmark

Every year, our team of six booksellers — with wildly different tastes — sits down, argues passionately, and eventually agrees. These are the ten books that defined 2026's literary landscape. We've left out the obvious chart-toppers and focused on books that genuinely moved us, challenged us, and stayed with us long after the final page.

"The best books of any year aren't the ones everyone talks about — they're the ones you press into a friend's hands at 9pm and refuse to leave until they promise to read it."

1. Orbital — Samantha Harvey

The Booker Prize winner that silenced the room when we first read it aloud. Harvey orbits the International Space Station for 24 hours, watching Earth turn below, confronting grief, beauty, and what it means to be a small creature in an enormous universe. Spare, luminous prose. A book that earns every accolade.

2. Intermezzo — Sally Rooney

Rooney's fourth novel is her most ambitious and, we think, her best. Two brothers navigate grief after their father's death, both stumbling through love in ways that are achingly human. She has always been skilled at writing desire; here she writes grief with equal precision.

3. James — Percival Everett

A radical retelling of Adventures of Huckleberry Finn from Jim's perspective that is both wildly entertaining and devastatingly serious. Everett dismantles American mythology with precision and wit. The Pulitzer Prize was well deserved.

Final Thoughts

If you only read one book from this list, make it Orbital. But honestly — read all of them. 2026 was an extraordinary year for literature, and these ten titles prove that the novel is in rude health.

Priya Nair

Priya is our Head Curator at The Shelf, specialising in literary fiction and South Asian literature. She has read every Booker Prize winner since 2005 and once stayed up until 4am to finish 'The God of Small Things' for the third time.
