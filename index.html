Our Heritage

A Century of Stories

Over a hundred years of gathering, restoring, and sharing human knowledge.

1920

The Genesis

Aurelia Books opened its doors as a quiet literary salon in a cobblestone lane, serving as a haven for the era's prominent writers, thinkers, and rare print collectors.

1954

Archival Restoration

Seeing a rise in damaged medieval prints, we established our first custom bindery and preservation workshop, hiring European master binders to conserve history.

1988

Global Scout Network

We expanded our reach, forming an international scout registry across six continents to trace and catalog lost manuscripts and certified first editions.

2026

The Digital Vault

Aurelia enters the modern age, introducing a bespoke digital portal that allows collectors globally to explore and request rare acquisitions from their private studies.

Common questions regarding acquisition, authentication, shipping, and library curation.

We recommend maintaining a temperature of 65–72°F (18–22°C) and relative humidity of 35–50%. Keep books away from direct sunlight, dust, and direct heaters. When handling rare volumes, ensure your hands are clean and dry, and avoid wearing cotton gloves which can snag vintage bindings.
